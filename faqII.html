Is drinking water a source of sodium?

Sodium is found in almost all foods (the main source of daily exposure) and in drinking water. Although sodium concentrations in drinking water are normally less than 20 mg / l, in some countries they can greatly exceed this amount. Some water softeners can significantly increase the sodium content of drinking water. If the concentrations exceed 200 mg / l, the water may have an unacceptable taste.

Is lactose-reduced milk easier to digest?

Low or reduced lactose milk is easier to digest only for people with lactose intolerance. If people whose body produces the enzyme lactase normally consume milk low or reduced in lactose for a long time, the body of these people will stop producing lactase because it does not need it and consequently it could develop lactose intolerance.

Do foods derived from GM crops cause cancer?

No. Cancer is a name applied to a spectrum of diseases in proliferating cells abnormally due to genetic and / or environmental factors, including tobacco smoke and UV rays from the sun, for example. There is no connection between GM crops and cancer, a burden of certain news that circulates.
